Eco Racer Game with AI Study

Alparslan Emrah Bayrak, PhD, from the School of Systems and Enterprises at Stevens Institute of Technology (SIT), is conducting a research study.

You were selected as a possible participant in this study because you meet the research criteria as a student at Stevens. Your participation in this research study is voluntary.

WHY IS THIS STUDY BEING DONE?

The primary objective of this research is to understand how usersâ€™ trust is formed in an Artificial Intelligence (AI) designed for a driving game and how trust impacts their decision-making based on how the AI system is designed.


WHAT WILL HAPPEN IF YOU TAKE PART IN THIS RESEARCH STUDY?

If you volunteer to participate in this study, the researcher will ask you to do the following:

  • You will play the online game.
  • This experiment will take place online and using your computer device.
  • We will ask you to play an online driving game supported by an AI.
  • We will ask you some questions about your trust in the AI in that game.
  • We will use the data from the game and your answers for analysis purposes.
    •


    ANTICIPATED LENGTH OF PARTICIPATION

    Participation will take a total of about 25 minutes.

    RISKS OR DISCOMFORTS

  • There are no anticipated risks or discomforts.


    • POTENTIAL BENEFITS


    You may benefit from the study in the following way(s):

    You will not directly benefit from your participation in the research.

    The results of the research may help researchers learn more about the factors that impact human interaction with intelligent systems. This information will help designers to develop intelligent systems that interact with users in a more intuitive way.


    WHAT OTHER CHOICES DO YOU HAVE IF YOU CHOOSE NOT TO PARTICIPATE?

    Your participation is completely voluntary. The alternative is not to participate in the study.


    SUBJECT COMPENSATION

  • There is no compensation for this experiment.


    • INFORMATION SECURITY.

    Data collected from this study will be confidential. Although your identity will be known, data is collected and maintained in the following ways in order to ensure confidentiality: You will be given a code name and in our data transcripts. Data analysis and presentation/dissemination will only use your code and will not include any identifiers. We will not collect or store any information that links your identity to your code name during the study. Your email address will be used to send you the possible gift card, and that information will not be linked to your data in any of the records we keep.


    PARTICIPANT RIGHTS

  • You can choose whether or not you want to be in this study, and you may withdraw your consent and
    discontinue participation at any time.
  • Whatever decision you make, there will be no penalty to you, and no loss of benefits to which you were
    otherwise entitled.
  • You may refuse to answer any questions that you do not want to answer and still remain in the study.
